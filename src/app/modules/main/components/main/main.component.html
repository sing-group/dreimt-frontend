What is DREIMT?

DREIMT is a bioinformatics tool for hypothesis generation and prioritization of drugs capable of modulating immune cell activity from transcriptomics data.

DREIMT integrates {{stats.drugCount | number}} drug profiles from The Library of Network-Based Cellular Signatures (LINCS) L1000 data set and {{stats.signaturesCount | number}} manually curated immune gene expression signatures from multiple resources to generate a drug-immune signature association database.

DREIMT can also prioritize drug associations from user-provided immune signatures.

DREIMT workflow

DREIMT integrates {{stats.drugCount | number}} consensus drug profiles data set and {{stats.signaturesCount | number}} manually curated immune gene expression signatures to generate pairwise drug profile-immune gene expression signature scores. Scores are prioritized to generate a drug profile-immune gene expression signature association score (tau). DREIMT uses tau score, FDR false discovery rate) and DSS (drug specificity score) to rank and generate hypotheses of drugs able to modulate immune gene expression signatures.
